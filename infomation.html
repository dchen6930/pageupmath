Right Triangle

Sine, Cosine and Tangent are the main functions used in Trigonometry

Sine, Cosine and Tangent can only be used in Right-Angled Triangle.

Sine, Cosine and Tangent ratio stays the same, Don’t matter on the size of the triangle

name to each side of a right triangle



"Opposite" is opposite(across from) to the angle θ or side θ

"Adjacent" is adjacent (next to) to the angle θ but is not the hypotenuse

"Hypotenuse" is the longest one or the is side that is opposite the right angle

How to calculate

Sine Function

sin(θ)= Opposite ÷ Hypotenuse

Cosine Function

cos(θ) = Adjacent ÷ Hypotenuse

Tangent Function

tan(θ) =Opposite ÷ Adjacent

Good calculators have sin, cos and tan on them, to make it easy for you. Just put in the angle and press the button.

How to remember? Think "Sohcahtoa"!

It works like this:

Soh...Sine = Opposite / Hypotenuse

Cah...Cosine = Adjacent / Hypotenuse

Toa...Tangent = Opposite / Adjacent

Inverse trig Functions


Inverse trig Functions are equal to 1 divided by cos, 1 divided by sin, and 1 divided by tan:

Secant Function:sec(θ) = Hypotenuse / Adjacent (=1/cos)

Cosecant Function: csc(θ) = Hypotenuse / Opposite (=1/sin)

Cotangent Function:cot(θ) = Adjacent / Opposite (=1/tan)
